# What to clean walls with?

Keeping your walls clean is an essential part of maintaining a beautiful and healthy home. Over time, walls accumulate dust, fingerprints, stains, and grime. Knowing **what to clean walls with** and how to do it properly prevents damage and keeps your home looking fresh. What to clean walls with?

### **1\. Start with Dry Cleaning**

Before using any liquids, remove loose dust and dirt from your walls:

-   Use a **microfiber cloth** or **duster** to gently wipe down the walls.
    
-   For higher walls, a vacuum with a brush attachment works well.
    

Dry cleaning prevents spreading dust and dirt when you move on to wet cleaning. Professional **house cleaning services near me** always start with this step for efficiency.

### **2\. Choose the Right Cleaning Solution**

Different wall types require different cleaning products:

-   **Painted walls:** Use a mild dish soap mixed with warm water. Avoid harsh chemicals that can strip paint.
    
-   **Wallpaper:** Use a soft sponge and mild soap solution; avoid soaking the wallpaper.
    
-   **Wood paneling:** Use a wood-safe cleaner or a mixture of water and vinegar in small amounts.
    

Always test a small area first to ensure the solution doesn’t damage the surface. **Professional deep cleaning services** know exactly which products work best for each wall type.

### **3\. Use Gentle Tools**

Avoid abrasive tools like scrub brushes or steel wool, which can scratch or damage walls. Instead, use:

-   Microfiber cloths
    
-   Soft sponges
    
-   Non-abrasive scrubbers
    

These tools remove stains effectively without harming paint, wallpaper, or wood finishes.

### **4\. Focus on Problem Areas**

High-traffic areas like hallways, door frames, and around light switches often accumulate fingerprints and smudges. Pay attention to:

-   Baseboards
    
-   Corners and edges
    
-   Areas near kitchen counters or sinks
    

Professional **home cleaning companies near me** provide detailed attention to these areas during deep cleaning sessions.

### **5\. Rinse and Dry**

After cleaning, rinse walls with a damp cloth to remove soap residue and prevent streaks. Always dry walls with a clean, soft towel to avoid water damage or streaking.
