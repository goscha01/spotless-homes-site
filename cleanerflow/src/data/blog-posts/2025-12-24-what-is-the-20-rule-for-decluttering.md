# What is the 20 rule for decluttering?

Decluttering your home can feel overwhelming, but the **20 rule** makes it simple and effective. ### **What Is the 20 Rule?**

The 20 rule for decluttering states: **If you haven’t used an item in the last 20 months, consider letting it go.** Some variations use weeks or years depending on the context, but the core idea is the same—focus on keeping only items you actively use or truly value. This simple rule helps homeowners avoid holding onto unnecessary clutter that takes up space and creates stress.

### **How to Apply the 20 Rule**

1.  **Go room by room:** Focus on one area at a time—closets, kitchen, living room, or bathroom.
    
2.  **Ask the 20-month question:** For each item, ask: “Have I used this in the past 20 months?”
    
3.  **Decide quickly:** If the answer is no, consider donating, selling, or recycling the item.
    
4.  **Organize the keepers:** Items you decide to keep should have a designated space for easy access and tidiness.
    

### **Benefits of the 20 Rule**

-   **Reduces clutter:** Frees up space for items you truly need and use.
    
-   **Eases cleaning:** Less clutter means faster and more efficient cleaning sessions.
    
-   **Improves mental clarity:** Organized spaces reduce stress and anxiety.
    
-   **Promotes mindful living:** Encourages intentional use of items instead of hoarding.

### **Extra Tips for Effective Decluttering**

-   **Set time limits:** Spend 15–30 minutes per session to avoid burnout.
    
-   **Use storage solutions:** Bins, baskets, and shelves help keep items organized.
    
-   **Start small:** Focus on high-impact areas like the kitchen counter or entryway.
    
-   **Maintain regularly:** Short, frequent decluttering sessions prevent clutter from building up again.
