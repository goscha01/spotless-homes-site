# What Is the Golden Rule for Toilet Cleaning? Should You Tip House Cleaners?

If there's one **golden rule for toilet cleaning**, it's this: **always clean from top to bottom**. This isn't just about efficiency—it’s about preventing cross-contamination and ensuring the most hygienic results.

![Cleaner disinfecting a toilet bowl with gloves and spray](/assets/blog/the-toilet-cleaning-golden-rule-clean-from-top-to-bottom-01.webp)

Toilet Cleaning Checklist

Here’s why this matters:

1.  **Start at the tank and flush handle.** These are the dirtiest places _you touch the most_.
    
2.  **Move to the lid and seat.** Use disinfectant and make sure you scrub the hinges.
    
3.  **Finish with the bowl and base.** Clean inside the bowl with a toilet brush and disinfectant, then wipe the outer base and floor area last.
    

This method prevents dragging bacteria from the bowl back to places you touch frequently—keeping your bathroom safer and fresher.

> 💡 **Pro Tip:** Use separate cleaning cloths for the toilet and the rest of the bathroom to prevent germ spread.

## 💸 Should You Tip Your House Cleaner?

Tipping your house cleaner isn’t always required, but it’s **greatly appreciated**, especially for:

-   **One-time cleanings**
    
-   **Move-outs or deep cleans**
    
-   **Holiday service**
    
-   **Exceptional service or last-minute requests**
    

### How much should you tip?

-   **Standard:** $10–$20 per visit
    
-   **Deep clean or larger homes:** $20–$50
    
-   **Recurring clients (monthly):** Tip at the end of the year or once per quarter
